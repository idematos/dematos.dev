Off the grid.

Since 2025, Guilherme and I have been living in a 6-metre-long campervan. We experiment with different ways of managing resources and waste outside the electricity, water and sewage grids. I will share some of what we have learned on these pages.

Story

In our mid-20s, we gradually began to live more simply and deliberately. By simply, I mean frugally, minimally, and closer to the wild. Deliberately, in this case, means making decisions based on our own values rather than convenience or external expectations. Even then, our sense of home and community was rather vague.

In 2022, we moved from Brazil to Malta, where we spent some time working remotely and began travelling more often. It was a great opportunity to learn languages, experience different cultures, and to develop empathy and curiosity. But we realised that simplicity requires a certain continuity, as constant change inevitably generates some form of waste.

So, seeking to balance mobility with stability, we decided to purchase a second-hand campervan that became our home for now. It gave us the flexibility to experiment with small-scale systems as we slowly move across Europe and, eventually, into Africa and Asia.
