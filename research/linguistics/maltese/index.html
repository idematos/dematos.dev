Bliet ta' Malta

0241226

Maltese is the only Semitic language written in the Latin script.

It is a unique language that evolved from Arabic while incorporating significant elements from Romance languages, particularly Italian and Sicilian, as well as English. It is the national language of Malta, and the only official Semitic and Afroasiatic language of the European Union.

Morphology and Writing System

Maltese has a root-pattern morphology where consonantal roots are combined with specific patterns to create words. However, it has also adopted simpler inflectional systems and vocabulary from Romance languages. Words are written in horizontal lines from left to right, like other Latin-based scripts.

Alphabet

The alphabet has 30 letters, including unique characters with diacritics like ċ, ġ, and ż, which represent sounds not found in most other Latin-script languages.

Vocabulary and Grammar

The core vocabulary, including pronouns and common verbs, often comes from Arabic roots, while words for modern concepts and formal terms are heavily influenced by Italian and English.

  • Arabic-origin words: dar (house), raġel (man), mara (woman).
  • Romance-origin words: skola (school), pulizija (police).
  • English-origin words: komputer (computer), televixin (television).

Phonology

The phonetic system includes pharyngeal and emphatic sounds, such as għ and ħ, which are unique among Latin-script languages. Stress in Maltese words generally falls on the penultimate syllable, though there are exceptions influenced by Romance borrowings.
