Maltese is a Semitic language written in the Latin script.

It is a unique language that evolved from Arabic while incorporating significant elements from Italian, Sicilian and English.

Alphabet

The alphabet has 30 letters, including unique characters with diacritics like ċ, ġ, and ż, which represent sounds not found in most other Latin-script languages.

Vocabulary and Grammar

The core vocabulary, including pronouns and common verbs, often comes from Arabic roots, while words for modern concepts and formal terms are heavily influenced by Italian and English.

  • Arabic-origin words: dar (house), raġel (man), mara (woman).

  • Italian-origin words: skola (school), pulizija (police).

  • English-origin words: komputer (computer), televixin (television).

Phonology

The phonetic system includes pharyngeal and emphatic sounds, such as għ and ħ, which are unique among Latin-script languages.
