Envy is a complex emotion and an often avoided topic.

To avoid bringing it, envy it is sometimes referred to as jealousy, but these are distinct emotions. Jealousy is the fear of losing something one possesses to another, while envy is a painful awareness of another's advantage, accompanied by a desire to possess that advantage or to see it diminished.

The Comparative Dimension

Envy is relational and comparative, meaning that it only arises in contexts where one measures their worth against another and, from the perspective of the envier, it is sufficient that the envied is in a slightly advantageous position relative to them.

The Moral Evaluation

Traditionally, envy has been classified as a vice, condemned for its corrosive effects on individual character and social harmony. Immanuel Kant counts envy among the "vices of misanthropy that are just opposite to human love", and Christian moral philosophy lists it among the seven deadly sins.

If envy is opposite to love, it is impossible for both emotions to coexist.
