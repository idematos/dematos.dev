In a determinate system, every event is causally inevitable.

'There's no free will', says the philosopher; 'To hang is most unjust'. 'There is no free will', assents the officer; 'We hang because we must'. Ambrose Bierce, Epigrams

Causal determinism is basically the idea that everything is predetermined and we have no control over our actions. This extends even further to human behavior, arguing that any action or choice someone makes is an inevitable consequence of genetic, neurological, and environmental factors.

The Contradiction

If each individual's actions are causally necessary, then concepts like justice and moral responsibility lose their meaning. How can we justify holding individuals accountable for their actions without assuming some degree of free will? Also, since determinism defends causality, the punisher would just follow the inevitable path set out for him as well. In this sense, I would argue that moral responsibility is human practices and emotions, which persists regardless of metaphysical considerations.
